## Background

Dave leverages his extensive life experience and previous professional
roles to develop a multi-faceted practice offering distinct services.

## Counsellor, therapist — and more

Dave is accredited as a counsellor and therapist for adults, and also
for young people from the ages of 11–18. He supports clients with
diverse concerns, ranging from abuse and addictions to sexual assault
and trauma — while also including Autism Spectrum Condition, ADHD and
neurodiversity generally.

The practice integrates coaching for both adults and young people
alongside counselling, enabling a richer and more diverse way to
explore and develop ways to support clients. Services extend to family
support and parenting skills for parents of children with behaviour
that is challenging, or who present with challenging behaviours.

## Counselling and Autism

The practice attracts clients from other counsellors, CAMHS and the
National Autistic Society, seeking support regarding Autism Spectrum
Conditions and neurodivergence.

Dave's expertise derives from personal experiences — teaching,
parenting and foster parenting, working for the National Autistic
Society — as well as an array of qualifications including a Master's
degree in Education (Autism), and Post Graduate qualifications in
Special Educational Needs and Psychological and Learning Disabilities.

## The setting

Sessions take place either in the garden office shed (face-to-face) or
online over a secure video link. The shed is a warm, confidential, and
safe space, surrounded by trees and the wildlife of the Surrey
countryside — deer, foxes, and a variety of bird life. Online
sessions mean Dave can support clients anywhere in the UK.
